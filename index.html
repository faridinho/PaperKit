What it does

Every PDF task researchers
actually need.

Five focused workflows, built around how academics actually use PDF files. Nothing bloated, nothing missing.

Smart PDF Renaming

PaperKit extracts the actual title from inside each PDF and renames the file automatically. No more download(3).pdf or arxiv_2301.07041.pdf in your library.

High-Quality Image Extraction

Pull every embedded figure, chart, and diagram out of a PDF at full resolution. Ideal for literature reviews, presentations, and reusing published visuals in your own work.

Metadata Export to Excel

Select any batch of papers and export their metadata — title, authors, year, keywords — directly into a structured Excel spreadsheet. Perfect for systematic reviews and citation tracking.

PDF Compression

Reduce file sizes significantly without visible quality loss. Send papers by email, upload to journal systems with upload limits, or keep your library from filling your drive.

Citation Export — RIS & BibTeX

Automatically generate .ris and .bib citation files from the metadata embedded in your PDFs. Import directly into Zotero, Mendeley, EndNote, or any standard reference manager.

How it works

Three steps.
That's genuinely it.

No setup, no account, no cloud. PaperKit runs entirely on your machine.

01

Drop your PDFs

Drag any number of PDF files into PaperKit, or point it at an entire folder. It handles batches of hundreds without breaking a sweat.

02

Pick your workflow

Choose what you need — rename, compress, export metadata, or extract images. Configure options if you want, or just run defaults. They're sensible.

03

Done. Originals safe.

PaperKit works on copies by default. Your original files are never touched. Results go exactly where you tell them. Nothing is sent anywhere.
